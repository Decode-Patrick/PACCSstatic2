GIS and SORADI Competence Centre for Development and Climate Change Adaptation

PACCS adopts a Socio-Ecological System (SES)-inspired mixed methods approach to triangulate qualitative research methods (qualitative interviews, focus group discussions, participant observation) with quantitative surveys and geographic information systems (GIS). GIS is uniquely suited for interdisciplinary data collection in the field of climate change as it captures and helps integrate data on variable localized impacts and adaptation strategies. In applying GIS we integrate geo-spatial tools, sketch maps and ground surveys.

PACCS moreover has supported the establishment of the Soradi Competence Centre for Development and Climate Change Adaptation, including a GIS lab, and by offering technical trainings in GIS proficiency and data management.
