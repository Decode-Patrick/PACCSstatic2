PACCS: Pastoralist Climate Change Resilience in Somaliland

Research-based Knowledge and Policy Impact

PACCS generates cutting-edge research and policy knowledge on pastoralist climate change resilience – and challenges to it. Conceptualizing pastoralism as a complex system, shaped by economic, socio-ecological and institutional factors, PACCS assesses pastoralist climate change resilience across the domains of markets, land management and hybrid governance arrangements in Somaliland. This cross-sectoral perspective allows PACCS to examine the previously understudied diversity of pastoralist adaptations, uncovering how different forms of climate change resilience (CCR) interact and their effects, and offering recommendations for policies and governance that better support pastoralism.

Current projects include:


  • ★ Durable Solutions for IDPs (project leader: Mohamed Fadal);
  • ★ Addressing Land degradation: Lessons learned from the Somaliland Development Fund’s projects (project leader: Nasir Ali);
  • ★ Rangeland management in Somaliland: Lessons learned from Aroori grazing reserve (Project leader: Ahmed I. Awale);
  • ★ Impacts of land enclosures (Project leader: Kedir Jemal);
  • ★ The role of women in climate- and resource related peace and conflict dynamics in Somaliland (Ayan Yusuf Ali, PhD candidate);
  • ★ Mapping climate governance in Somaliland and ways forward for pastoral climate resilience (Project leader: Sharmaarke Musse);
  • ★ Market-oriented adaptations of pastoralists in Somaliland Project leader: Abdi Osman Haji;
  • ★ Inclusive and sustainable pastoral development in Somaliland in the context of climate change: Market based and economic pathways (Roble Abdi Muse, PhD Candidate).
